# [us] — portrait mapping framework
## artist brief + selection specification

*This document serves two purposes:*
*1. Artist brief — defines the visual language, archetypes, and tonal direction for commissioned artwork*
*2. Selection spec — defines how the match engine maps signal clusters to portrait images*

---

## overview

Every user who completes the [us] intake receives a portrait at Block 8. The portrait has two parts:

1. **Written portrait** — [you] reflects back what it observed across the conversation. Spoken aloud in [voice]. 4–8 sentences. Second person. Ends with an open door.

2. **Visual portrait** — a single image selected from a curated library of 25–50 original illustrations. The image is not literal. It is not a picture of the user. It is a visual metaphor — chosen because something in it resonates with who this person is right now.

The image does not explain itself. It simply lands.

Users can accept their portrait image or ask [you] for a different one from the same archetype cluster. They cannot browse the full library. The selection feels curated, not chosen from a menu.

---

## visual language — locked direction

**Medium:** Digital illustration. Not photography. Not AI generation. Original commissioned artwork.

**Tone:** Atmospheric. Intimate. Quiet. Each image should feel like a moment caught rather than composed — as if the viewer arrived just in time to see something true.

**Color:** Muted, deep palettes. No primary colors used boldly. Light is the subject in many images — how it enters a room, reflects off water, fades at a threshold. Consistent color sensibility across the library so all 25–50 images feel like they belong together, even across different archetypes.

**Scale:** Some images are vast (horizon, sky, open water). Some are close (a hand, a doorway, a single lamp). Both scales should be represented in the library.

**People:** Figures may appear but should be anonymous — seen from behind, partially obscured, or abstracted. The viewer should be able to place themselves in the image, not observe someone else.

**What to avoid:**
- Literal or illustrative depictions of connection (two people holding hands, etc.)
- Bright, optimistic, or commercial aesthetics
- Anything that reads as generic wellness or self-help imagery
- Faces
- Text or symbols within the image

## the seven archetypes

Each archetype maps to a dominant signal cluster from the portrait engine. Images are commissioned per archetype — roughly 4–7 images each across 25–50 total.

---

### 1. ROOTED
*Signal cluster: High values signal + low narrative urgency + strong completion signal in Block 2*

**Who this is:** Someone who knows what they stand for. They are not in a hurry. They protect what matters and do not apologize for it. Their stability is not stagnation — it is foundation.

**Visual direction:**
Something ancient. Something that has been here a long time and will remain. The image should convey depth rather than height, weight rather than movement.

- Old growth tree seen from below, canopy reaching out of frame
- Stone archway, worn smooth, light passing through
- Still water reflecting sky — the surface undisturbed
- A room that has been lived in for decades — books, low light, settled
- Roots visible above ground, massive, tangled, alive

**Tonal quality:** Quiet confidence. Not dramatic. Not melancholic. Simply present.

---

### 2. HORIZON
*Signal cluster: High narrative signal + high aspiration language + strong Block 3 completion + urgency/directional language detected*

**Who this is:** Someone in motion. They know where they are going, even if they cannot name it precisely. They are building something. The future is not abstract to them — it is close.

**Visual direction:**
Expansive. Directional. The eye should naturally move toward something — a vanishing point, a threshold, an opening. These are images of becoming.

- Coastline at dawn, water meeting land, light just arriving
- Open road disappearing into distance, late afternoon
- A window open to a wide sky, curtain moving slightly
- Figure seen from behind, standing at the edge of something vast
- First light over a city seen from a height

**Tonal quality:** Anticipation without anxiety. Forward without rushing.

---

### 3. INTIMATE
*Signal cluster: High relational signal + attachment warmth indicators + strong Block 5 completion + caretaker or depth-seeker language*

**Who this is:** Someone for whom connection is everything. They give deeply. They notice small things. They are most themselves in close proximity to another person. Depth over breadth, always.

**Visual direction:**
Close. Warm. Contained. These images are about the space between two things — not the things themselves. Light sources are single and warm. Scale is human or smaller.

- Two chairs facing each other, one lamp between them
- A hand resting on a table, light falling across it
- A doorway between two rooms — one lit, one dark
- Letters or books, well-worn, evidence of return
- A threshold — the moment just before entering

**Tonal quality:** Warmth without sentimentality. Presence without performance.

---

### 4. CURRENT
*Signal cluster: High communication directness + conflict engagement (Block 4 accepted and engaged) + active repair language + high OCEAN conscientiousness*

**Who this is:** Someone who moves through the world with energy and intention. They engage rather than avoid. They say the hard thing. They are not afraid of friction — they know it is how things get resolved.

**Visual direction:**
Movement. Energy that is directed, not chaotic. These images should feel alive — something is happening, something is flowing or burning or pushing forward.

- River moving fast over rocks, shot close to the surface
- Fire contained — a hearth, a candle in wind, a controlled burn
- Wind through tall grass, everything bending in the same direction
- Rain on glass, motion blur, city lights diffused
- A forge or kiln — heat, transformation, something being made

**Tonal quality:** Alive. Purposeful. Not aggressive — energized.

---

### 5. LIMINAL
*Signal cluster: High OCEAN openness + exploratory narrative language + Block 4 accepted + comfort with ambiguity detected + multiple connection types indicated*

**Who this is:** Someone who lives comfortably in the in-between. They are not lost — they are exploring. They hold uncertainty without needing to resolve it. They are often the most interesting person in the room precisely because they have not decided yet.

**Visual direction:**
Threshold states. The moment between one thing and another. Fog, reflection, transition. These images should feel like questions rather than answers.

- Fog over water in early morning, the far shore not quite visible
- A reflection in water that is almost but not quite accurate
- A doorway or gate, open, leading somewhere not fully seen
- Dusk — the exact moment between day and night
- A path that curves out of sight, what's ahead unknown

**Tonal quality:** Curious. Open. Comfortable with not knowing.

---

### 6. CELESTIAL
*Signal cluster: High astrological weight (user engaged deeply with cosmological layer) + contemplative language throughout + Block 1 arrival was unhurried + low urgency, high meaning-making*

**Who this is:** Someone who understands their life as part of something larger. They find meaning in pattern, in cycle, in the long view. They are not detached — they are oriented. The cosmos is not abstract to them; it is context.

**Visual direction:**
Vast and quiet. Night. Light that has traveled far to arrive here. These images should make the viewer feel small in a way that is comforting rather than diminishing.

- Night sky, stars visible, horizon low
- A single light source seen from a great distance
- Moon on water, the path of reflection leading toward the viewer
- A telescope or observatory at night, open to the sky
- Constellation — not a diagram, but the actual sky, the actual stars

**Tonal quality:** Vast. Quiet. Grounded in something larger than the self.

---

### 7. COMPOSITE
*Signal cluster: Balanced signal distribution across layers — no single dominant archetype. Scores within 15% of each other across values, narrative, relational, and communication layers.*

**Who this is:** Someone who holds multiple qualities without resolving them. They are not one thing. The engine cannot easily characterize them — and that is itself a characterization. These are often the most interesting portraits.

**Visual direction:**
Images that hold more than one quality simultaneously. Something that is both intimate and vast, both rooted and in motion, both clear and obscured. These are the hardest images to make and the most important.

- A tree at the edge of the sea — rooted and facing horizon simultaneously
- A lit window in a vast dark landscape — intimate scale, enormous context
- A river that widens into an estuary, losing its definition — current becoming something larger
- A figure at a window, inside looking out — threshold, intimacy, horizon all at once
- Dawn breaking over still water — celestial light, intimate reflection, liminal moment

**Tonal quality:** Cannot be reduced to one word. That is the point.

## selection algorithm — technical spec

### step 1: score each archetype

After Block 8 intake completion, the portrait engine scores the user against all seven archetypes using the following signal weights:

```
ROOTED:     values_signal(0.4) + stability_language(0.3) + low_urgency(0.3)
HORIZON:    narrative_signal(0.4) + aspiration_language(0.35) + urgency_language(0.25)
INTIMATE:   relational_signal(0.4) + attachment_warmth(0.35) + caretaker_language(0.25)
CURRENT:    communication_directness(0.3) + block4_engagement(0.35) + repair_language(0.35)
LIMINAL:    ocean_openness(0.35) + ambiguity_comfort(0.35) + multi_connection_type(0.3)
CELESTIAL:  astrological_weight(0.4) + contemplative_language(0.35) + low_urgency(0.25)
COMPOSITE:  triggered when no archetype scores >15% above the mean
```

### step 2: select dominant archetype

- If one archetype scores ≥ 25% above mean → select that archetype
- If two archetypes are within 10% of each other at the top → blend (see step 3)
- If no clear dominant → COMPOSITE

### step 3: handle blended archetypes

When two archetypes are close, select an image from the library tagged with both archetypes. Every image in the library is tagged with a primary archetype and optionally a secondary archetype. Blended selection prioritizes images tagged with both.

### step 4: image selection within archetype

Each archetype has 4–7 images. Selection within the archetype is semi-random with one constraint: users who return for a second portrait (after significant connection history) should not receive the same image twice.

### step 5: user response

After image is presented:
- User accepts → portrait locked, NFT mint initiated if user opts in
- User asks for another → [you] selects next image in same archetype cluster
- User asks why this image → [you] gives a brief, honest answer rooted in what it observed (never reveals the archetype name or scoring logic)

## nft specification

**Chain:** Solana
**Mint price:** 0.1–0.2 SOL (~$15–30). Not free — price signals that the portrait means something.
**Royalties:** 5–7% on secondary sales. OMARO keeps mint revenue and royalties.
**Contents:** Portrait image + written portrait text + portrait metaphor + timestamp + anonymized user ID
**Ownership:** User holds the key. Fully transferable.
**Framing:** "This is who you were on [date]. It belongs to you."
**Metadata fields:**
  - `portrait_image`: IPFS hash of illustration
  - `portrait_text`: written portrait (full text)
  - `portrait_metaphor`: the metaphorical bridge text tying written portrait to visual
  - `archetype`: primary archetype name
  - `secondary_archetype`: if blended, the secondary archetype
  - `minted_at`: ISO timestamp
  - `platform`: "[us] by OMARO PBC"

**Minting trigger:** User opts in after portrait is delivered and confirmed. Never automatic.
**Wallet:** User provides Solana wallet address OR [us] generates a custodial wallet for non-crypto users with clear export instructions.

## nft collection — hybrid model

**Collection size:** 5,000–10,000 unique pieces
**Model:** Hybrid — commissioned originals multiplied and diversified by AI

**Workflow:**
1. Commission artist → 25–50 original atmospheric illustrations (the visual foundation — human-made, style-locked)
2. Feed originals into Hotpot.ai as style reference → generate thousands of variations (same palette, same atmosphere, diversified compositions)
3. Result: large collection that feels hand-crafted because it started that way
4. Deploy via AutoMinter → Solana chain, mint button embedded directly in [us] app
5. Secondary market: Magic Eden (Solana-native) + OpenSea

**Tool stack:**
- **Hotpot.ai** — style-consistent variation generation from commissioned originals (~$250 for 5,000 images)
- **AutoMinter** — collection deployment, minting, mint button embed in [us] app (free, pay gas only)
- **Magic Eden** — primary secondary marketplace (Solana-native)
- **OpenSea** — secondary marketplace (broader reach)

**Relationship to app:**
The 25–50 commissioned originals are the in-app portrait library.
The 5,000–10,000 AI-generated variations are the NFT collection.
They share visual DNA but serve different purposes — the app delivers the most resonant original, the NFT project scales the universe of that image into something collectible.

Holding an NFT from the collection unlocks paid features in [us]. The NFT project funds the platform.

## artist brief summary

**What we need:** 25–50 original digital illustrations across 7 archetypes (roughly 4–7 per archetype), plus 5–8 blended images tagged across two archetypes.

**Style:** Atmospheric, muted palette, intimate scale mixed with vast scale, no faces, figures anonymous when present, consistent visual language across the full library.

- High resolution (3000px minimum on shortest side)
- Two crops: square (for NFT) and 4:3 landscape (for in-app display)
